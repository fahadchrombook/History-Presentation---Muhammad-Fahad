This presentation explores major global conflicts after WWII.
Key events, causes, outcomes, and impacts are explained concisely.

Post World War II Tensions

After WWII, the world divided into the US and USSR superpowers. Europe was split politically and ideologically. NATO and the Warsaw Pact were formed. Nations aligned with either capitalism or communism. Tensions sparked proxy wars worldwide. Reconstruction of Europe began alongside military buildup. These tensions set the stage for the Cold War.

The Iron Curtain & Berlin Wall

Europe divided with the "Iron Curtain" between East and West. East Germany under Soviet control, West Germany democratic. Berlin was split, leading to tension and migration issues. The Berlin Wall was built in 1961 to prevent escapes. It became a global symbol of the Cold War. Walls and checkpoints defined daily life in Berlin. Its fall in 1989 marked Cold War decline.

The Korean War (1950–1953)

North Korea invaded South Korea in 1950. UN forces, led by the US, intervened. China supported North Korea militarily. Battles occurred around the 38th parallel. Armistice signed in 1953, leaving Korea divided. Millions of military and civilian casualties. Set precedent for Cold War conflicts in Asia.

The Vietnam War (1955–1975)

North Vietnam aimed to unify under communism. US supported South Vietnam to stop communist expansion. Guerilla warfare and bombings devastated the region. Millions of lives were lost. Anti-war protests rose globally. Saigon fell in 1975; North Vietnam victorious. Conflict left long-lasting social and political impact.

Rwanda Genocide (1994)

Ethnic tensions between Hutu and Tutsi exploded. Extremists orchestrated mass killings across Rwanda. 800,000 people were killed in 100 days. Refugees fled to neighboring countries. Global response was delayed and insufficient. It highlighted the dangers of ethnic hatred. Became a key case study in humanitarian law.

First Gulf War (1990–1991)

Iraq invaded Kuwait, creating global crisis. US-led coalition launched Operation Desert Storm. Airstrikes and ground forces liberated Kuwait. Modern military technology demonstrated power. Iraq suffered heavy losses. Regional tensions remained post-war. Oil and economy influenced international policy.

War on Terror

9/11 attacks by Al-Qaeda led by Osama bin Laden shocked the world. US launched counter-terrorism campaigns globally. Afghanistan invaded to remove Taliban & Al-Qaeda. Security and intelligence operations expanded worldwide. Allied countries joined anti-terror missions. Terrorist networks disrupted but threats persisted. War reshaped global security and policy.

Second Gulf War (Iraq 2003)

US invaded Iraq to remove Saddam Hussein. Iraqi government collapsed; insurgency rose. Coalition forces conducted reconstruction and security missions. Sectarian violence destabilized the region. International debate over war justification ensued. Long-term effects shaped Middle East politics. Conflict continued for years after invasion.

Afghanistan Wars

USSR invaded in 1979; withdrew 1989 due to resistance. Taliban took control in 1990s. US invaded in 2001 to combat Taliban & Al-Qaeda. NATO and allies participated in military operations. Afghanistan faced long-term instability. Reconstruction struggled amid insurgency. Wars shaped global counter-terrorism policies.

Ongoing Israel-Palestine Conflict

Violence continues between Israel and Palestinian groups. Territorial disputes and political tensions persist. Periodic clashes cause casualties and destruction. Humanitarian crises draw international attention. Peace talks have struggled to succeed. Conflict remains unresolved globally. The region experiences continuous instability.

Conclusion & Lessons

Conflicts post-WWII shaped global politics and human history. Wars caused immense suffering but taught lessons. Studying events helps understand diplomacy and peace. Modern conflicts are interconnected worldwide. Awareness and education are key to prevent future wars. Peace, dialogue, and collaboration are essential. History guides us to build a better, stable future.
